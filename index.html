About Me

I obtained a B.Sc. degree in Computer Engineering at University of Pernambuco (Brazil) in 2006. Then, I received a M.Sc. in Computer Engineering at the Aeronautics Institut of Aeronautics (Instituto Technologico de Aeronautica - ITA) in 2009. In 2013, I received a Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at University of Nantes, thanks to my contributions related to the coordination of Autonomic Cloud Services. From 2013 to 2015, I worked as a post-doctoral fellow at INRIA Grenoble (France) on applying control-theoretical approaches to reconfigurable software components. Since mid 2015, I have been working as a research/lecture associate at Ecole des Mines de Nantes (France). My current research interests include the application of of concepts of software engineering (architectures, models and languages) and control theoretical approaches (e.g., discrete controller synthesis) to ease the development and management of Self-adaptive Software Systems.
